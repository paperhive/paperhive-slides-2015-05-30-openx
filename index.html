Academia today

  • Publishing: paywall vs. open access
  • Participation: backroom science vs. open science
  • Reproducibility: closed vs. open data/source
  • Reputation and measurability: impact factor vs. diverse metrics
  • Collaboration: traditional communication vs. internet

Collaboration problems

You read a paper and

  • you don't understand what's going on
  • you think you found a mistake
  • there's a mistake and you didn't find it (but someone else did)
  • you know a better paper about the topic
  • you discovered an improvement and would like to contribute it to the original paper
  • you'd like to discuss the paper with colleagues and friends

Enter PaperHive

The Look

What can you do with PaperHive?

  • Review and improve
    • share your opinion on-the-fly
    • add helpful formulas, figures, data, code, ...
  • Discuss and learn
    • ask and answer questions
    • ignite fruitful conversations
  • Annotate and discover
    • attach your comments where they belong
    • find relevant comments immediately

Shared and structured discussions instead of unassociated sticky notes!

A network of papers and researchers

  • Currently: network of papers established via backward references by the authors at time of publication
  • With PaperHive:
    • comments may link to other papers, code, data, ...
    • forward references, finally!
    • constantly evolving network of papers established by authors & readers

Open and citable

  • Public comments are licensed under Creative Commons BY license (CC-BY-4.0)
  • Each comment
    • has its own URL
    • can be cited
    • can be reused (with proper attribution)

Our data is your data – API

  • open REST API for accessing public comments
  • everyone can build tools on top of the PaperHive infrastructure
    • enrich metrics
    • show comments wherever you want (e.g., on journal websites)
    • data/graph analysis
  • API documented on docs.paperhive.apiary.io

Free & libre open source software

  • Free software (GPL v3)

The Future

Arriving soon

  • Notifications for authors and tracked papers

  • Linked data via JSON-LD, Open Annotation data model

  • Private comments (personal or for a group)
  • Allow other formats than PDFs
  • Content moderation (spam, trolls, ...)
